## 4. Overlap and duplication

**Three taste-battle clones.** `selectr`, `selectd`, and `canonise` ship near-identical READMEs ("competitive multiplayer music taste evaluation platform where users drop tracks, battle head-to-head"). `dropr` is a fourth competitor in the same space ("the arena where taste is proven"). Pick one (recommend `dropr` — sharpest prose; `canonise` builds a pipeline ladder with `canora`). Kill `selectr` + `selectd` + `canonise`, or merge them into a single taste-battle product. **Save: ~3 codebases, 1 brand.**

**Slayt vs Tessera.** `tessera/README.md` is byte-equivalent to `Slayt/README.md` (Atelio framing, 1193 genome line). Tessera is a duplicate. Delete or archive.

**Stanvault vs Convicta vs Fanora-MVP.** All three claim the superfan identity / fan score lane. CLAUDE.md has anointed `stanvault` (branded Imprint) as canonical. `convicta` overlaps with its own "Impact Power" framing and global focus, and `fanora-mvp` is the older MVP. Recommend: **kill or rebrand convicta into a Stanvault enterprise tier**, and archive `fanora-mvp`.

**Palmlion vs Oryx vs Hawkleo.** Per CLAUDE.md, Palmlion + Oryx are merging; `hawkleo` reads as a third version of the same thing ("Built on Oryx's conviction engine"). Three African-music conviction apps is two too many. **Pick one consolidated brand.**

**Crucibla vs Squarp vs Imperium vs Issuance.** Each owns a distinct stage (decision → handoff → settlement → registry), but `issuance` overlaps with `imperium` for "audio as digital art with provenance + chain settlement." Recommend: fold `issuance` into Imperium as a luxury-tier product surface, or kill it.

**Subtaste vs Subtaste-twelve vs Boveda-Subtaste-Fusion.** Subtaste is canonical. The other two are older iterations. Archive.

**Tizita vs Clarosa.** Confirmed already (CLAUDE.md). `clarosa` is empty `.vite` scaffold. Delete folder; keep DB column prefix migration on the queue.

**Twin-os-core vs Starforge.** `twin-os-core` predates Starforge (raw Python + sqlite). Archive.

**Ubani vs Ubani-release-manager.** Latter is empty `.next`. Delete.

**Sankore vs Qualn.** Sankore is the port-target of qualn. Run the port; archive `qualn` after Phase 3 completes.

**Boveda vs Genoma vs Boveda-Subtaste-Fusion vs Living-Character-OS.** Multiple character-genome and visual-DNA repos. Boveda is the main lane. The fusion folder is research; genoma is visual-DNA-for-fashion (different lane). Keep Boveda + Genoma; archive the fusion repo.

**Ossivale vs Species8-plugin.** Ossivale is documentation/architecture mirror of Species8. Keep one canonical source; archive ossivale.

**Vocal instruments cluster.** `Maud`, `SYNDRAVA`, `Meredith`, `HEAT_TERRAIN`, `Feedback`, `VocalRelicMachine`, `peri` all live in Max/MSP. Not duplicates — each has a distinct sonic identity — but worth consolidating under a single "VIOLET SPHINX Instruments" brand (currently undeclared) so they don't fragment GTM.

## 5. Gaps (no app coverage)

1. **Manager / label A&R roster intelligence.** No app helps a manager or label scout, score, and track signed and unsigned artists at portfolio level. Stanvault is fan-side; Crucibla is artist-side; nothing is roster-side.
2. **LatAm and India geography.** Africa is heavily covered (Palmlion, Oryx, Hawkleo, Dasham). LatAm (Brazil/Mexico) and India (200M+ Spotify+JioSaavn users) have zero coverage.
3. **Mastering / mix advice.** Species8 mutates; nothing reviews a finished mix and gives loudness/translation guidance comparable to iZotope Tonal Balance or LANDR.
4. **Sync placement marketplace.** Musea is a Codex prompt only. SINK is consent-side. No app brokers actual sync deals music-supervisor-side.
5. **Fan-to-fan secondary market.** Stanvault verifies; Imperium settles. Nothing facilitates fan resale of fractional or merch tokens.
6. **Live event / ticketing tied to Stan Score.** StanVault → Imperium contract exists for token gating; no app handles ticketed shows or guest-list logic.
7. **Cross-app analytics dashboard.** CLAUDE.md mentions a `ecosystem_users` table; no app surfaces it as a UI yet.
8. **Lyric-to-licensing pipeline.** Ibis writes lyrics; nothing converts a lyric draft into a publishing/PRO/sample-clearance workflow.
9. **Fake-artist (AI) gatekeeping for DSPs.** Canora gates internally; no B2B product yet sells this gatekeeping to Spotify/Apple Music.

## 7. Cross-cutting observations

- The ecosystem has converged around a clear "post-vanity" thesis. Pain points around **conviction**, **verified humans**, **post-streaming**, and **on-chain settlement** are over-served (good for thesis defence, bad for resource allocation).
- The ecosystem is under-served on the **B2B label / manager** side, **non-African emerging markets**, and **mastering / engineering quality**.
- Multi-app convergence opportunities: Slayt + Tessera + Atelio; Selectr + Selectd + Canonise + Dropr; Stanvault + Convicta + Fanora; Palmlion + Oryx + Hawkleo. Consolidating these would cut 9 codebases without losing pain-point coverage.
- Most-trustworthy single integration thread, end-to-end: **Tizita → Starforge → Slayt → Folio → Subtaste → Stanvault → Imperium**, with **Crucibla → Squarp → Imperium** as the artist-release thread, and **Palmlion/Oryx → Stanvault** as the fan-conviction thread. Everything else is either an instrument, an experiment, or a duplicate.
